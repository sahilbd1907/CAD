CNC Cutting Guide

An overview of CNC cutting processes, materials, and best practices to help you prepare files for accurate quotes and quality results.

Processes
  • Laser Cutting: High precision for metals and plastics. Minimal kerf, clean edges.
  • Plasma Cutting: Fast cutting for thicker metals. Wider kerf, heat-affected zone.
  • Waterjet Cutting: No heat-affected zone. Suitable for metals, composites, stone.
  • Router/End Mill: Ideal for wood, plastics, aluminum; good for pockets and profiles.
Materials & Thickness

Common materials include steel, aluminum, brass, copper, wood, and plastics. Thickness impacts feed rates, tool selection, and cost. Ensure you select the correct thickness during quote generation.

Design Guidelines
  • Use single, continuous outlines where possible to minimize tool lifts.
  • Maintain minimum feature sizes relative to tool diameter and material.
  • Account for kerf width; allow adequate clearance for fits.
  • Remove duplicate/overlapping entities in DXF to avoid double cutting.
File Preparation (DXF)
  • Export 1:1 scale in millimeters, 2D profiles only.
  • Place part outlines on a single layer; remove dimensions and title blocks.
  • Join segments to polylines; avoid splines where possible.
  • Verify no open contours for profiles intended to be cut through.
